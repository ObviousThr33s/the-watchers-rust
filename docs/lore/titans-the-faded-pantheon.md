# Titans — the faded pantheon

*A seed. Parked, not judged — no finish line yet.*

## The kernel

Of the six Titan brothers, only two kept their own weather:

- **Oceanus** — the world-encircling sea. The name does what it says.
- **Hyperion** — "the high one," Titan of light; father of Helios, Selene, Eos.

The other three were sanded down by the myths until they survived only as
fathers-of-the-famous — names without portfolios:

- **Coeus** — the celestial pole / inquiry; grandfather of Apollo & Artemis through Leto.
- **Crius** — the constellations / the southern pillar; almost no myths of his own.
- **Iapetus** — mortality and craft; sire of Prometheus, Epimetheus, Atlas, Menoetius.

And **Cronus**, the ringleader, kept *all* his menace — which is exactly why
forgetting him stings.

## The beat

To be forgotten is to be reduced to lineage. To be remembered — feared — is to
keep your teeth.

## Why it belongs here

This is the occlusion law read backwards. The engine's conceit is *things change
when unwatched*. The Titan pattern names the **cost** of going unwatched: you
don't just move in the dark, you *erode* — portfolio first, then identity, until
you're a footnote whose only remaining job is to sire the next thing.

Vesh is "seen only when they allow it." A faded Titan is the inverse — **seen, it
might be restored; unseen, it dwindles to genealogy.**
