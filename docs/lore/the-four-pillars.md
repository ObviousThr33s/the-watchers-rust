# The four pillars

*A seed. Parked, not judged — no finish line yet.*

## The kernel

Before the count of six, there's a count of four. In one reading, four Titans
hold up the sky at the corners of the world — **Coeus** (north), **Crius**
(south), **Hyperion** (east, the dawn), **Iapetus** (west, the mortal lands).
When the Titans fall, Iapetus's son **Atlas** inherits the weight. The corners
are load-bearing; pull one and the sky tilts.

This is the "four" that's easy to half-remember instead of six — see
`titans-the-faded-pantheon.md`.

## The beat

Some things are not characters but corners. You don't meet them; you stand inside
them. You only notice a pillar when it's the one that's gone.

## Why it belongs here

This is the lore-shadow of the engine's **load-bearing wards** (CLAUDE.md — the
zero-reference payload, the bounded queue, deferred dispatch: *"break one and the
whole occlusion/timing model rots."*) The four pillars are the narrative version
of that same law: invisible structure whose only tell is the catastrophe when it
fails. The dread is structural — not a monster in the room, but a pillar quietly
going.

## Possible uses (undecided)

- Four anchor-entities or sites that keep a region coherent; as each falls, the
  occlusion rules in that region degrade — more drift, more erosion.
- Cardinal framing for region/level layout: N/S/E/W as Coeus/Crius/Hyperion/Iapetus.
- A late beat where the player becomes **Atlas** — forced to hold one corner
  themselves, unable to look away because looking away is what lets it fall.
