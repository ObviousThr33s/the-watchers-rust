# Mnemosyne & Lethe — the two rivers

*A seed. Parked, not judged — no finish line yet. The physics behind the fade.*

## The kernel

**Mnemosyne** is memory — a Titaness, mother of the Muses; to be held in her
water is to keep your name and your shape. **Lethe** is the underworld river the
dead drink to dissolve their old lives. The faded Titans
(`titans-the-faded-pantheon.md`) are what happens when Lethe wins slowly: not
death, just erosion to a name.

## The beat

Nothing here truly dies. It is either remembered into shape or forgotten into
lineage. Decay is only which river you're standing in.

## Why it belongs here

If "decay while occluded" ever becomes a mechanic, these two are its units.
Watching — being named — draws from Mnemosyne: a being keeps its portfolio.
Occlusion, neglect, is Lethe: the portfolio dissolves, behavior by behavior,
until only the husk and its name remain. It gives the fade a measurable substance
— a single axis from Lethe to Mnemosyne — instead of an on/off flag.

The flora already gesture at it: the **Elder** is *"the oldest,"* remembered so
long the clearing keeps its distance.

## Possible uses (undecided)

- A memory/oblivion scalar per being: high = full behaviors, art, and line;
  low = glyph and name only, the rest stripped.
- Watching as a slow refill toward Mnemosyne; absence as a drift toward Lethe.
- A persistence flavor: what the world "remembers" between runs is whatever stayed
  above the Lethe line.
